## DESCRIPTION OF THE PROBLEM

The image shows superficial cosmetic damage on the front left corner of the car: visible scuffing and paint transfer/scratches on the bumper area near the headlight and wheel arch. The headlight lens itself appears intact, with no obvious cracking or breakage, but there are clear abrasion marks along the painted surface and trim.

### Possible causes:

- Light side-swipe or contact with another vehicle
- Scraping against a post, wall, curb, or parking barrier
- Minor impact while maneuvering in a tight parking space
- Road debris or brush contact causing surface abrasion

### Possible consequences:

- Cosmetic deterioration and reduced appearance
- Potential clear-coat/paint damage leading to future corrosion if the underlying layer is exposed
- Reduced resale value
- If hidden impact occurred, possible misalignment of bumper/headlight mounting points

### Possible measures to prevent it:
- Add protective clear film or bumper corner protection in high-risk areas
- Improve parking aids such as parking sensors, cameras, or automated parking assistance
- Increase bumper corner scratch resistance through tougher coating systems
- Design wider clearance margins around protruding front corners and wheel-arch interfaces
- Use flexible, impact-absorbing trim or sacrificial exterior molding in vulnerable zones
