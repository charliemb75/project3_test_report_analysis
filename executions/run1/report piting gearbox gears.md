## DESCRIPTION OF THE PROBLEM

The image shows significant surface wear on metal gear teeth, with polished/scuffed contact bands, discoloration, and localized roughening consistent with abrasion, sliding wear, or early pitting/scuffing. The damage appears concentrated on the working flanks of the teeth, suggesting repeated metal-to-metal contact under load and possibly inadequate lubrication or misalignment.


### Possible causes:
- Insufficient lubrication or lubricant breakdown
- Gear misalignment causing edge loading
- Excessive load or shock loading
- Contaminants in the lubricant (abrasive wear)
- Improper gear tooth profile or manufacturing errors
- Heat treatment or surface hardness issues
- Poor surface finish increasing friction


### Possible consequences:
- Increased noise and vibration
- Reduced gear efficiency
- Progressive wear leading to tooth profile change
- Scuffing progression or pitting
- Overheating of the gear set
- Risk of tooth cracking or failure
- Shortened service life of the transmission/system


### Possible measures to prevent it:
- Ensure proper lubrication type, level, and supply method
- Improve filtration and contamination control
- Verify gear alignment and bearing support during assembly
- Optimize tooth surface finish and profile accuracy
- Use appropriate surface hardening/heat treatment
- Design for adequate load capacity and safety margin
- Control operating temperature and avoid overload conditions
