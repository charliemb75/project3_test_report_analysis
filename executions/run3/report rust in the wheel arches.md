# RUST IN THE WHEEL ARCHES

## GENERAL DATA\n\n
- **Models affected:** M02, M01
- **Parts affected:** P0006, P0007, P0008, P0009, P0020
- **Last modification:** 21/05/2026

## DESCRIPTION OF THE PROBLEM\n\n
The image shows severe corrosion and rust-through along the rear wheel arch/body panel, with the paint and protective coating lifting away and the metal visibly degraded. The rust has progressed from surface corrosion to structural perforation at the arch edge, indicating long-term moisture exposure and coating failure.\n\n
### Possible causes:\n\n
- Stone chips or scratches that damaged the paint and exposed bare metal\n
- Trapped moisture and road salt accumulating in the wheel arch\n
- Failed or thin factory corrosion protection / undercoating\n
- Poor drainage or blocked mudguard/arch cavities\n
- Prior body repair or filler work that was not properly sealed\n
- Prolonged exposure to wet environments without prompt repair\n\n
### Possible consequences:\n\n
- Further spread of rust into adjacent body panels\n
- Loss of structural strength in the wheel arch area\n
- Water ingress into inner panels and cabin/trunk areas\n
- Damage to nearby components from corrosion debris and moisture\n
- Potential failure of mounting points or trim attachment areas\n
- Reduced vehicle value and inspection/roadworthiness issues\n\n
### Possible measures to prevent it:\n\n
- Apply robust corrosion-resistant coatings and seam sealers in wheel arch areas\n
- Use galvanized or corrosion-resistant sheet metal in fabrication\n
- Ensure proper drainage and avoid moisture traps in panel design\n
- Add stone-chip protection and durable paint systems on exposed edges\n
- Design wheel arch liners and splash guards to reduce salt and water exposure\n
- Inspect, clean, and repair coating damage early before rust spreads
