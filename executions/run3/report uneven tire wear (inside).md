- **Parts affected:** P0030
- **Last modification:** 21/05/2026

## DESCRIPTION OF THE PROBLEM

The image shows severe tire tread wear, with the center of the tire significantly more worn than the outer tread blocks. The tread depth appears low in the center, indicating uneven wear consistent with overinflation, long-term use, or alignment/suspension issues. The tire may be approaching unsafe service limits due to reduced grip and exposed wear patterns.

### Possible causes:

- Overinflation causing accelerated center tread wear
- Improper wheel alignment, especially toe/camber misalignment
- Extended driving with insufficient tire rotation
- High highway mileage with consistent straight-line wear
- Suspension or steering component wear affecting tire contact patch
- Incorrect tire load or pressure maintenance over time

### Possible consequences:

- Reduced traction, especially in wet or slippery conditions
- Increased risk of hydroplaning
- Longer braking distances
- Higher chance of tire failure or blowout due to reduced tread depth
- Uneven handling and reduced vehicle stability
- Premature tire replacement and increased maintenance cost

### Possible measures to prevent it:

- Maintain tire pressure according to manufacturer specifications
- Perform regular wheel alignment checks and corrections
- Rotate tires at recommended intervals
- Inspect suspension and steering components for wear
- Match tire load rating and usage to the vehicle’s operating conditions
- Design tires and recommend materials/compound profiles that improve wear uniformity
