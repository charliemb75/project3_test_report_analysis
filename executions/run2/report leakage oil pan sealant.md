# LEAKAGE OIL PAN SEALANT


## GENERAL DATA


- **Models affected:** M02

- **Parts affected:** P0004

- **Last modification:** 20/05/2026


## DESCRIPTION OF THE PROBLEM

A close-up of a car underbody component shows a fastener/plug area with visible dark staining around the bolt head, suggesting fluid seepage or accumulation of oily grime at the joint. The surrounding surface is dusty and there are signs of wear or possible corrosion/discoloration near the fastener seat, indicating a localized sealing or fastening issue.

### Possible causes:

- Loose or improperly torqued fastener
- Worn or damaged sealing washer / gasket / O-ring
- Oil, grease, or other fluid leakage from the nearby assembly
- Corrosion or contamination around the fastener seat
- Vibration-induced loosening over time
- Previous improper installation or repair

### Possible consequences:

- Progressive fluid leakage
- Reduced lubrication or loss of hydraulic function, depending on the part
- Contamination of nearby components with oil/dirt buildup
- Corrosion spread or material degradation
- Fastener loosening further, potentially leading to component damage
- Increased maintenance needs and risk of part failure

### Possible measures to prevent it:
- Apply correct torque during assembly
- Use the specified sealing washer, gasket, or thread sealant
- Improve corrosion-resistant coatings on the fastener and mating surfaces
- Design for better sealing at the joint interface
- Use vibration-resistant fastening methods, such as locking features
- Implement quality control checks for installation and leakage testing
