# RUST IN THE WHEEL ARCHES


## GENERAL DATA


- **Models affected:** M02


- **Parts affected:** P0006, P0007, P0008, P0009


- **Last modification:** 20/05/2026


## DESCRIPTION OF THE PROBLEM

Severe corrosion and rust-through are visible along the rear wheel arch and lower quarter panel. The paint has blistered and flaked away, exposing heavily oxidized metal with material loss, indicating advanced body panel deterioration rather than just surface rust.

### Possible causes:
- Prolonged exposure to moisture, road salt, and debris
- Damage to paint/coating allowing corrosion to start
- Trapped dirt and water inside the wheel arch
- Poor drainage or inadequate seam sealing
- Previous minor impact or scraping that broke the protective layer
- Long-term neglect of early rust spots

### Possible consequences:
- Continued spreading of rust into adjacent body panels
- Loss of structural strength in the affected area
- Holes forming in the wheel arch and quarter panel
- Water intrusion into inner body cavities and trunk area
- Damage to nearby trim, liners, and suspension-area components
- Reduced vehicle value and more expensive body repair

### Possible measures to prevent it:
- Apply durable anti-corrosion coatings and seam sealers in wheel arch areas
- Use better paint systems with strong chip resistance
- Improve drainage and avoid water traps in panel design
- Add protective liners or stone guards in high-splash zones
- Use galvanized or corrosion-resistant materials for body panels
- Repair paint chips and scratches quickly before rust can start
- Design overlapping joints to minimize exposed edges and moisture retention
